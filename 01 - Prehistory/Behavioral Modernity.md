# Behavioral Modernity

Behavioral modernity refers to the suite of cognitive and cultural capabilities that distinguish *Homo sapiens* from earlier homininsâ€”symbolic thought, art, complex tools, long-distance trade, and organized society. When and how our ancestors became "fully modern" in behavior remains debated: Was it a sudden revolution ~50,000 years ago, or a gradual accumulation over hundreds of thousands of years?

## Overview

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Definition | Full suite of modern human behaviors |
| Timing | Debated: 100 ka gradual vs. 50 ka revolution |
| Location | Africa (earliest evidence) |
| Markers | Art, symbolism, complex tools |

## The Debate

### Revolution Model (Old View)

| Claim | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Timing | ~50,000 years ago |
| Location | Europe or Near East |
| Mechanism | Genetic mutation ("human revolution") |
| Evidence | Upper Paleolithic explosion |

### Gradualist Model (Current)

| Claim | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Timing | Gradual, starting 100+ ka |
| Location | Africa |
| Mechanism | Cultural accumulation |
| Evidence | African Middle Stone Age |

## Why Africa First?

| Factor | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| Anatomical modernity | First in Africa (~300 ka) |
| Population | Largest, most diverse |
| Time depth | Longest behavioral record |
| Evidence gap | Preservation, research bias |
