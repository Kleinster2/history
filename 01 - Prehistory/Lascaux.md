# Lascaux Cave

Lascaux is perhaps the most famous prehistoric cave art site in the world—a complex of caves near the village of Montignac in southwestern France containing nearly 600 paintings and 1,500 engravings dating to approximately 17,000 years ago. Often called the "Sistine Chapel of Prehistory," Lascaux represents the apex of Ice Age artistic achievement.

## Conservation Crisis

### Opening to Public

| Year | Visitors | Effect |
|------|----------|--------|
| 1948 | Opened to public | — |
| 1955 | 1,200/day | Deterioration begins |
| 1960s | Peak tourism | Serious damage |
| 1963 | CLOSED | To save the art |

### The "Green Sickness"

| Problem | Cause |
|---------|-------|
| Algae growth | CO2, humidity from visitors |
| Bacterial mats | Introduced microorganisms |
| Calcite veils | Moisture changes |
| Fungal outbreaks | 2001, 2007 ongoing |

### Conservation Efforts

| Measure | Description |
|---------|-------------|

| Air conditioning | Climate control |
