# Dmanisi

Dmanisi is a remarkable archaeological site in the Republic of Georgia that has produced the oldest hominin fossils found outside Africaâ€”1.8-million-year-old remains of early *Homo erectus*. The exceptional preservation of five skulls and associated postcranial bones has revolutionized our understanding of the first human dispersal from Africa.

## The Hominins

### Five Skulls

| Skull | Age/Sex | Brain (cc) | Notable Features |
|-------|---------|------------|------------------|
| D2280 | Young adult | 650 | Complete female |
| D2282 | Adult | 775 | Large, robust |
| D2700 | Adolescent | 600 | Small, juvenile |
| D3444 | Old adult | 650 | Toothless, survived |
| D4500 | Adult male | 546 | Largest face, smallest brain |

### Skull 5 (D4500)

The most complete early *Homo* skull:

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Discovery | 2005 |
| Publication | 2013 |
| Brain size | 546 cc (smallest *erectus*) |
| Face | Large, projecting |
| Jaw | D2600 (massive mandible) |
| Significance | Shows extreme variation |

| Raw material | Local basalt, quartzite |

### Significance

| Issue | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| No handaxes | Left Africa before Acheulean |

## Significance

### What Dmanisi Proves

| Finding | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| Early dispersal | 1.8 Ma, very early |
| Small brains | ~600 cc average |
| Small bodies | Not tall, robust |
| Simple tools | Oldowan sufficient |
| Variation | Species-level differences in one population |
