# Ardipithecus

Ardipithecus represents some of the earliest known homininsâ€”ancient human relatives living near the split between the human and chimpanzee lineages. "Ardi," the famous skeleton of *Ardipithecus ramidus*, transformed our understanding of early human evolution, revealing that our ancestors did not evolve from chimpanzee-like creatures but from something different entirely.

## Anatomy

### Bipedalism

| Feature | Interpretation |
|---------|---------------|
| Pelvis | Adapted for upright walking |
| Foot | Big toe divergent (grasping) |
| Mix | Bipedal on ground, climbed trees |
| Implication | Not fully committed biped |

### Other Features

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|

| Hands | Less specialized than chimps |
| Teeth | Reduced canines |

## Environment

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Habitat | Woodland, forest |
| Not savanna | Challenges old theories |

## Significance

| Discovery | Implication |
|-----------|-------------|
| No knuckle-walking | Humans didn't evolve from chimp-like ancestor |
| Woodland biped | Bipedalism not savanna adaptation |
| Mosaic evolution | Different traits evolved separately |
| Last common ancestor | Probably unlike modern apes |
