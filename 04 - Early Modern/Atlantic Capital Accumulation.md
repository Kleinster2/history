# Atlantic Capital Accumulation

How did slave trade wealth become factory capital? The [[Atlantic Economy|Atlantic economy]] didn't just transfer wealth—it built the financial institutions that enabled industrial capitalism.

## The Williams Thesis

### Eric Williams's Argument (1944)

| Claim | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Core thesis | Slavery profits funded [[Industrial Revolution|industrialization]] |
| Mechanism | Capital accumulated from slave trade → invested in industry |
| Examples | Liverpool merchants, Bristol banks, London finance |
| Political | Abolition came when slavery became unprofitable |

### The Debate

| Position | Evidence |
|----------|----------|
| **For Williams** | Individual fortunes traceable to slavery |
| | Slave ports became industrial centers |
| | Timing correlation |
| **Against Williams** | Slave trade profits small vs. GDP |
| | Most capital came from agriculture, domestic trade |
| | Abolition preceded decline |

## The Deeper Point

The Atlantic economy didn't just transfer wealth—it built the [[Institutions and Progress|financial institutions]] (insurance, banking, credit, risk assessment) that enabled industrial capitalism. Whether slave profits "caused" industrialization is less important than recognizing how deeply the systems were intertwined.

The cotton that fed Lancashire mills was grown by enslaved people. The ships that carried goods were insured by Lloyd's. The credit that financed voyages came from banks built on Atlantic trade. The demand for manufactures came partly from plantation economies. Separating "clean" industrial capital from "tainted" slave wealth may be impossible—the systems grew together.

This doesn't mean slavery was economically necessary for industrialization—counterfactuals are unknowable. But it does mean that the [[Industrial Revolution]] emerged from a specific Atlantic system where slavery, empire, and industry were interconnected. The institutions built for managing the risks and financing the trade of Atlantic slavery became the institutions of modern capitalism.
