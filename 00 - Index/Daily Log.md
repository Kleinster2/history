## 2026-03-20

- [[Lebanese Banking Crisis]] (`05 - Modern/`) — new topic note. Full arc from 1956 banking secrecy law (capital flight from Nasser's nationalizations → 85 banks by 1960, "Switzerland of the Middle East") through civil war destruction (1975-90), Taif Agreement confessional power-sharing, Hariri's reconstruction borrowing (debt past 100% GDP by 1997), Riad Salameh's dollar peg (LL 1,507.5 from 1997), the Ponzi machine (BdL paying above-market rates on dollar deposits funded by new inflows, banking assets reaching $217B / 4x GDP, 71% deposit dollarization), 2016 "financial engineering" (banks earned ~$5B, BdL cost ~$13B), political-banking nexus (18 of top 20 banks had politically connected shareholders, 43% of assets controlled by elites), October 2019 collapse (WhatsApp tax protests, informal capital controls without legal authority), first sovereign default (March 2020), Beirut port explosion (August 2020, 218 dead, $15B damage), "lollarization" (dollar deposits worth 16 cents on the dollar by 2024), lira losing 98% of value, GDP contracting 58% (World Bank: top 10-15 worst since 1850s), poverty reaching 85-90%, Salameh investigations across six European countries. Deeper Point: purest modern example of sovereign Ponzi finance in Minsky's sense — confessional democracy made resolution impossible because no faction could impose losses on another; the system designed to prevent civil war made economic governance unachievable. Historical irony: Beirut's role as financial hub echoes the Levant's ancient function as trade junction, but medieval ports extracted rents from real goods while modern banking extracted rents from financial flows with no underlying production.

## 2026-03-19

- [[Helium Economy]] (`05 - Modern/`) — new topic note. Full arc from 1868 spectral discovery (Janssen/Lockyer, element found on the Sun before the Earth) through 1895 Ramsay isolation (Nobel 1904), 1905 Kansas gas well, WWI strategic awakening (Petrolia experimental plant 1915), Bureau of Mines monopoly (Amarillo plant 1929, sole global producer by 1934), Hindenburg as consequence of U.S. helium embargo, WWII production surge, 1960 conservation era (425-mile federal pipeline, Bush Dome stockpile to 1B+ cubic meters), 1996 Privatization Act (Congress liquidates strategic reserve to retire $1.4B debt), four shortage cycles (2006-2023), and Qatar concentration risk. Deeper Point: democratic governance failure on exhaustible resources — the SPR survived because oil shortages are politically visible; helium's reserve died because its users (MRI, semiconductors) were invisible to voters. By the time helium became critical to AI chip fabrication, the buffer was gone. Cross-linked to investing vault's 2026 crisis note.

## 2026-03-17

- [[Walid Khalidi]] (`People/`) — new person note. Jerusalem-born Palestinian historian (1925–2023) from the prominent al-Khalidi family. Oxford-educated, taught at AUB and Harvard, co-founded the Institute for Palestine Studies (1963). Life work: reconstructing the economic and social reality of Palestine before 1948. *All That Remains* (1992) documented 418 villages depopulated in the 1948 war — populations, crops, infrastructure, fates — using Mandate census data, Ottoman tax registers, and aerial photographs. *Before Their Diaspora* (1984) assembled the photographic record. Economic angle: displacement as destruction of accumulated capital (agricultural, human, institutional, social); the Khalidi family as multi-generational institutional capital dispersed in a generation; diaspora investment in education as portable capital; historiography as implicit economic argument against "empty land" narratives.

## 2026-03-16

Gilded Age people and philanthropy: four new notes covering the Rockefeller/Carnegie wealth-and-redistribution arc.

- [[John D. Rockefeller]] (`People/`) — new person note. Cleveland bookkeeper → Standard Oil monopoly (90% of refining by 1880) → Cleveland Massacre, railroad rebates, predatory pricing → Tarbell exposé (1904) → Supreme Court breakup into 34 companies (1911) → breakup paradox: held shares in all fragments, wealth peaked at ~$900M → Rockefeller Foundation (1913), $540M total philanthropy. Often called richest American ever. Full-arc lede, Deeper Point on whether monopoly and progress are compatible.
- [[Andrew Carnegie]] (`People/`) — new person note. Scottish immigrant (father ruined by steam looms) → bobbin boy → telegraph → Pennsylvania Railroad under Scott → Bessemer steel → vertical integration (ore to rail) with Frick → Homestead Strike 1892 (Pinkertons, union crushed) → Gospel of Wealth doctrine → sold to Morgan for $480M (1901, U.S. Steel) → $350M philanthropy (2,509 libraries, Carnegie Hall, Carnegie Mellon) → died 1919 with WWI having shattered his peace idealism. Deeper Point on the tension between accumulation and redistribution.
- [[Ida Tarbell]] (`People/`) — new person note. Oil Region childhood (father's business destroyed by Standard Oil) → Allegheny College → Paris → McClure's Magazine → *The History of the Standard Oil Company* (19 installments, 1902-04): documented rebates, drawbacks, predatory pricing, espionage from court records and insider sources → built political case for 1911 breakup → established investigative journalism as institutional check on economic power. Suffrage paradox noted.
- [[Gospel of Wealth]] (`Themes/`) — new thematic note. Carnegie's 1889 *North American Review* essay: inequality is natural, the rich are trustees, "the man who dies thus rich dies disgraced." Three dispositions of wealth (heirs/bequest/lifetime giving). Strategic dimension: preempted calls for confiscation by proposing voluntary redistribution. Homestead contradiction. Enforcement problem → Progressive Era answer: 16th Amendment and estate tax. Legacy through Gates-Buffett Giving Pledge.

Medieval trade commodity notes: four new thematic/commodity notes on medieval trade systems.

- [[Spice Trade]] (`Themes/`) -- new thematic note. The highest-value long-distance commerce of the pre-modern world, traced from Roman pepper imports through Arab middlemen, Venetian monopoly, Portuguese sea route, and Dutch VOC monopolization. Covers markup ratios (100:1 source to consumer), key spices (pepper, cinnamon, cloves, nutmeg), the chokepoint pattern (whoever controls the terminus extracts rent), and how Venice's success drove the Age of Exploration. Connects to Venice, Levant Trade, Indian Ocean Trade, East India Companies, Age of Exploration.
- [[Levant Trade]] (`03 - Medieval/`) -- new note. The eastern Mediterranean coast as the critical junction where Asian luxury goods entered European markets. Covers Crusader-era Italian merchant colonies (Venice, Genoa, Pisa quarters in Acre, Tyre, Alexandria), the goods flowing through (spices, silk, dyes, sugar), the fall of Acre (1291) and its aftermath, Venice's systematic monopolization, Genoa's Black Sea alternative, and the Ottoman capture that motivated Portuguese route-finding. Argument: the Levant as geographic chokepoint -- positional, not productive value.
- [[Woolen Cloth Trade]] (`Themes/`) -- new thematic note. Medieval Europe's most important manufactured export, covering the full international supply chain: English sheep pastures → Flemish looms (Bruges, Ghent, Ypres) → Florentine finishing (Arte della Lana, 30,000+ workers) → Mediterranean markets. Covers the putting-out system as proto-industrial production, the Ciompi revolt (1378), and the connection between wool finance and Florentine banking (Bardi/Peruzzi collapse). Distinct from existing Wool Trade note, which covers the English raw wool export side.
- [[Grain Trade]] (`Themes/`) -- new thematic note. The "boring" bulk commodity that enabled everything else. Two systems: Mediterranean grain (Sicily, Egypt, Black Sea → Italian cities) and Baltic grain (Poland/Prussia via Danzig → Hanseatic ships → Amsterdam). Covers Venice's terraferma expansion as food security, the Teutonic Order as grain exporter, Amsterdam's *moedernegotie*, and the grain trade's dark consequence -- the Second Serfdom in Eastern Europe.

Iran arc expansion: four new notes building out the oil-sovereignty and modernization threads.

- [[OPEC]] (`05 - Modern/`) — new note. Baghdad founding (1960) by Venezuela, Saudi Arabia, Iran, Iraq, Kuwait as defensive response to Seven Sisters' price cuts. Arc: first decade irrelevance → 1973 embargo as oil weapon → price revolution (10x, 1970-80) → nationalization wave transfers 90% of reserves to NOCs → 1980s collapse (conservation, non-OPEC supply, Saudi overproduction) → internal rivalries (Saudi vs. Iran/Iraq, Iran-Iraq War, Kuwait invasion) → OPEC+ with Russia (2016). Cartel dynamics, price-takers to price-makers, rents redistribution from IOCs to NOCs. Member state table.
- [[Petrodollar System]] (`Themes/`) — new analytical/thematic note. Post-1973 system: oil priced in USD → OPEC surpluses → US Treasuries/Western bank deposits → banks lend to developing countries → Third World debt crisis. Covers US-Saudi 1974 deal, Eurodollar expansion, Wriston's "countries don't go bankrupt," recycling scale ($375B cumulative 1974-81), de-dollarization pressures. Argument: transformed oil from commodity market into financial architecture that propped up dollar after Bretton Woods collapse.
- [[1953 Iranian Coup]] (`05 - Modern/`) — new note. CIA/MI6 overthrow of Mossadegh, August 1953. Arc: Mossadegh nationalizes AIOC (1951) → British embargo → Truman refuses → Eisenhower/Dulles reframe as anti-communist → Operation Ajax first attempt fails, Shah flees → second attempt succeeds → Shah restored → consortium (40% US, 40% UK, 20% others). Irony: Iran got the 50-50 deal it originally sought, minus its democracy. Template for Guatemala, Congo, Chile. 26-year fuse to revolution.
- [[White Revolution]] (`05 - Modern/`) — new note. Shah's top-down modernization (1963-1979). Six reforms expanded to 19: land reform (~50% of arable land redistributed), Literacy Corps (illiteracy halved), women's suffrage, industrialization. GDP growth 9-11% annually. Unintended consequences: displaced peasants, destroyed landlord class, threatened bazaaris, clergy lose waqf lands → Khomeini's opposition begins June 1963 (arrest, exile). Paradox: material success without political infrastructure → revolution.

- [[Mohammad Mossadegh]] (`People/`) — new note. Qajar aristocrat, Swiss-educated lawyer, Iranian PM 1951-53. Nationalized Anglo-Iranian Oil Company (BP), triggering British global boycott → economic strangulation → CIA/MI6 Operation Ajax (August 1953) overthrew him. Restored the Shah to absolute power. 3 years prison, 14 years house arrest, died 1967. Economic angle: first major test of resource sovereignty vs. international capital — legally sound, democratically supported, but failed without market access or collective action. 1953 became founding grievance for the revolution 26 years later. OPEC learned from his failure: cartels succeed where solo nationalization fails.
- [[Shah Mohammad Reza Pahlavi]] (`People/`) — new note. Ruled Iran 38 years (1941-79). Installed by Allies at 21 → humiliated by Mossadegh → restored by CIA coup → White Revolution (1963): land reform, literacy, women's suffrage, industrialization → OPEC price hawk (drove 1973 quadrupling) → GDP growth 34% in 1974 → but SAVAK repression, corruption, inflation, no political institutions → revolution 1979 → exile → died Cairo 1980. The definitive petro-state paradox: GDP per capita tripled, literacy quadrupled, military largest in Middle East — yet overthrown by nearly all social classes. Oil eliminated the need for consent; modernization without institutions is building on sand.
- [[Ayatollah Khomeini]] (`People/`) — new note. Shia cleric who built the world's first modern theocracy. Opposed Shah's White Revolution 1963 → exiled 15 years (Iraq, France) → returned Feb 1, 1979, greeted by 3 million → promised democracy, delivered theocracy → velayat-e faqih → exploited hostage crisis to destroy moderates → prolonged Iran-Iraq War 6 years beyond territorial recovery → 1988 prison massacres (2,800-5,000 killed) → died 1989. "Economics is for donkeys": bonyads as corrupt conglomerates, brain drain of 2-3 million, autarky as ideology. Created the system so thoroughly that Khamenei — whom he publicly humiliated — could inherit it.
- [[Qasem Soleimani]] (`People/`) — new note. IRGC Quds Force commander 1998-2020. Village boy → Iran-Iraq War volunteer → rose through IRGC → built the "axis of resistance": Hezbollah (~$700M/year), Hamas, Houthis, Iraqi Shia militias, saved Assad in Syria (personally persuaded Putin to intervene, July 2015). Proxy network doubled as sanctions-evasion economic infrastructure. Killed by US drone strike Baghdad airport Jan 3, 2020. Death created coordination gap: no successor had personal relationships with all proxy leaders → proxies freelanced → October 7th chain of miscalculations.

- [[Republic of Genoa]] (`03 - Medieval/`) -- new note. Full-arc city-state note covering Genoa as Pisa's destroyer and Venice's rival. Arc: Byzantine-era port --> First Crusade (1097-99) --> Crusader state trade privileges --> rivalry with Pisa over Sardinia/Corsica --> Battle of Meloria (1284) destroys Pisan fleet --> Black Sea colonial empire (Caffa, Galata, Treaty of Nymphaeum 1261) --> War of Chioggia with Venice (1378-81) --> Casa di San Giorgio (1407) as proto-central bank --> Genoese bankers finance Spanish Empire ("Age of the Genoese," Braudel) --> Columbus --> decline under foreign domination --> Napoleon dissolves republic (1797). Economic angle: Genoa vs. Venice institutional comparison (entrepreneurial dynamism vs. institutional stability), maona as proto-joint-stock company, trade-to-finance pivot as recurring pattern, alum monopoly, Black Sea slave trade.
- [[Amalfi]] (`03 - Medieval/`) -- new note. First Italian maritime republic. Arc: Byzantine dependency --> independent duchy (839) --> first Italian merchants in Islamic world (Fatimid Egypt trade) --> Tabula Amalfitana (maritime law code, ~11th c.) --> Hospitaller connection (founded Jerusalem hospice ~1023) --> Norman conquest (1073) --> sacked by Pisa (1135-37) --> permanent decline. Economic angle: pioneered the Italian merchant model but couldn't scale it (cliffside city, ~30,000 population, no harbor expansion); first-mover advantage without institutional durability; gold tari as first Christian-Italian gold coin.

- [[Iranian Revolution]] (`05 - Modern/`) — new note. Shah's prosperous but unfree Iran → Khomeini's consolidation via hostage crisis → hardliner feedback loop (Iranian actions empower opposing hawks, whose responses empower Iranian hardliners) → Khamenei's rise as underdog who militarized politics through IRGC → economic consequences (oil shock, nationalization, brain drain, middle class erosion from ~65% to 5-10% core support by 2020s). Two factors keep regime in place despite unpopularity: no exit ramp for elites, no organized alternative.
- [[Iran-Iraq War]] (`05 - Modern/`) — new note. Saddam invades 1980 expecting quick win (Putin/Ukraine parallel). 8-year trench war with chemical weapons, Iran fights alone ("strategic solitude"). Forges IRGC, missile program, proxy doctrine. First war in 250 years Iran doesn't lose territory. Paradoxically consolidates revolutionary regime. Post-war reconstruction contracts → IRGC economic empire.
- [[IRGC]] (`05 - Modern/`) — new note. Parallel army created because revolutionaries distrusted Shah's US-trained military. Name has no "Iran" — safeguards the revolution, not the nation. Khamenei made it his power base after watching Soviet reform-collapse. Economic empire: bonyads, construction, telecoms, oil, sanctions evasion. Soleimani assassination (2020) removed coordinating hand → proxy freelancing → October 7th miscalculation chain.
- [[JCPOA]] (`05 - Modern/`) — new note. Obama's pivot from "huge pressure" to secret Burns-Sullivan talks in Oman. Deal: 97% stockpile shipped out, half centrifuges dismantled, unprecedented inspections. Breakout time 12+ months. Theory of change: 5% growth → middle class expansion 65%→85% → generational transition. "Good news: nuclear deal. Bad news: only nuclear deal." Trump withdrew May 2018. Breakout collapsed to 6 days by Jan 2025. Kushner-Witkoff negotiations lacked technical competence; Iranians were desperate for deal, came with working papers, willing to give more than Obama got.
- [[Iran Sanctions]] (`Themes/`) — new note. 45-year escalation: Carter freezes → Clinton ILSA → Obama SWIFT cutoff → Trump maximum pressure. Sanctions paradox: weakened middle class, strengthened IRGC (smuggling networks, government contracts, hard currency control = profit from isolation). Pattern of broken promises (Algiers, hostage reciprocity, Clinton oil contract, JCPOA relief, Biden prisoner deal) reinforced hardliner narrative. Comparative cases: Cuba, North Korea, Iraq, Russia — sanctions rarely achieve regime change in authoritarian states with alternative networks.
