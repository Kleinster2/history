# Thutmose III

Thutmose III (reigned 1479-1425 BCE) was ancient Egypt's greatest warrior pharaohâ€”the "Napoleon of Egypt" who conducted 17 military campaigns and created an empire from Nubia to the Euphrates. His victory at Megiddo established Egyptian dominance in Syria-Palestine for a century. The wealth flowing from conquest and tribute transformed Egypt's economy, funding temples and supporting an expanded bureaucracy.
