# Mediterranean Trade

The Mediterranean Sea—the center of ancient world commerce—connected three continents and dozens of cultures through maritime trade. From Phoenician merchants to Roman grain fleets to Venetian galleys, the inland sea facilitated exchange that shaped civilizations. Mediterranean trade patterns established economic relationships that persisted for millennia, making the sea a crucible of commercial innovation.
