# Old Kingdom (2686-2181 BCE)

The Old Kingdom, spanning Dynasties 3-6, represents ancient Egypt's first golden age and is often called the "Pyramid Age." During this period, the pharaonic state reached its first peak of centralized power, producing the Great Pyramids of Giza and establishing the model of divine kingship that would define Egyptian civilization for millennia.

## The Pyramid Age

### Development of Pyramids

| Stage | Pharaoh | Innovation |
|-------|---------|------------|
| Mastaba | Pre-Djoser | Flat-topped tombs |
| Step Pyramid | Djoser | Stacked mastabas |
| Bent Pyramid | Sneferu | Angle change mid-build |
| Red Pyramid | Sneferu | First true pyramid |
| Great Pyramid | Khufu | Largest ever |

### Giza Complex

| Pyramid | Pharaoh | Height | Base |
|---------|---------|--------|------|
| Great Pyramid | Khufu | 146.6 m | 230.4 m |
| Khafre's | Khafre | 143.5 m | 215.3 m |
| Menkaure's | Menkaure | 65.5 m | 102.2 m |
| Sphinx | Khafre (?) | 20 m | 73 m long |

### Pyramid Construction

| Theory | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Ramps | Internal or external |
| Labor | Organized workforce, not slaves |
| Materials | Local limestone, Tura casing |
| Organization | Rotating crews, provisioned |
| Duration | ~20 years per pyramid |
