# Mohenjo-daro

Mohenjo-daro ("Mound of the Dead") was the largest city of the Indus Valley Civilization and one of the world's earliest major urban centers. At its peak around 2000 BCE, it housed 30,000-40,000 people in a meticulously planned city with the ancient world's most sophisticated sanitation system.
