# Deir el-Medina

Deir el-Medina was an Egyptian village of tomb workersâ€”the craftsmen who carved and decorated the royal tombs in the Valley of the Kings. What makes it extraordinary is preservation: thousands of ostraca (pottery shards) and papyri record daily life in detail. We know these workers' wages, their strikes for back pay, their lawsuits, and their love letters. It's ancient history's best window into working-class life.

## The First Recorded Strike

### The Event (Year 29 of Ramesses III, ~1156 BCE)

| Event | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Cause | Wages 18 days late |
| Action | Workers left work |
| Protest | Sat at temple gates |
| Demands | Owed rations |
| Resolution | Eventually paid |
