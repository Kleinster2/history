# Bronze Age Collapse

The Bronze Age Collapse (~1200-1150 BCE) was the ancient world's great catastropheâ€”the simultaneous collapse of the interconnected civilizations of the eastern Mediterranean. Within decades, the Hittite Empire fell, Mycenaean Greece collapsed, Egyptian power contracted, and cities across the Levant were destroyed. Trade networks dissolved, writing disappeared from Greece, and population declined sharply. This collapse demonstrates how interconnected economies can fail together.

| -------------- | --------------------------- |
| [[Iron Age]]   | New technology              |
| Alphabet       | [[Phoenicians\|Phoenician]] |
| New powers     | [[Ancient Israel]], Phoenicia, later Greece |
| Trade recovery | Centuries later             |

The collapse was creative destruction. The palace economies had to fail for new systems to emerge: Phoenician traders filled the commercial vacuum, Israelites established themselves in the Canaanite power void, and Greece eventually re-emerged with city-states instead of palaces.
