# Delian League

The Delian League began as a defensive alliance against Persia and became the Athenian Empireâ€”a system of tribute extraction that funded Athens' golden age. What started with voluntary contributions to a common treasury at Delos ended with forced payments to Athens. The League's transformation from alliance to empire illustrates how economic power creates political dominance.

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Founded | 478 BCE |
| Original purpose | Defense against Persia |
| Members | ~150-200 city-states |
| Treasury | Delos, then Athens (454 BCE) |
| End | 404 BCE (Sparta's victory) |
