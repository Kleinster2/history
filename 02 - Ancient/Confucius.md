# Confucius

Confucius (Kong Qiu) was China's most influential philosopher—his teachings shaped Chinese society, government, and values for over two millennia. Confucianism became the ideological foundation of the Chinese state, defining proper relationships, ethical governance, and social harmony. Understanding Confucius is essential to understanding why Chinese institutions developed differently from Western ones.

## Core Ideas

### Ren (仁) - Humaneness

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Meaning | Benevolence, humanity |
| Scope | Concern for others |
| Practice | Empathy, kindness |
| Centrality | Highest virtue |

> "What you do not wish for yourself, do not do to others."

### Li (禮) - Ritual Propriety

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Meaning | Ritual, proper conduct |
| Scope | All social interactions |
| Function | Creates harmony |
| Source | Ancient traditions |

### The Five Relationships

| Relationship | Principle |
|--------------|-----------|
| Ruler-subject | Loyalty and benevolence |
| Parent-child | Filial piety |
| Husband-wife | Distinction, care |
| Elder-younger | Respect |
| Friend-friend | Faithfulness |

## Political Philosophy

### Good Government

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Moral example | Ruler's virtue transforms people |
| Meritocracy | Promote the worthy |
| Ritual | Proper ceremonies |
| Light rule | Minimal coercion |

> "If you lead with virtue and regulate with ritual, the people will have a sense of shame and will correct themselves."

### The Mandate of Heaven

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Principle | Heaven grants right to rule |
| Condition | Depends on virtuous governance |
| Loss | Bad rulers lose mandate |
| Revolution | Justified against tyrants |

### Confucius vs. Legalists

| Feature | Confucianism | Legalism |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| Human nature | Improvable | Selfish |
| Government | Moral example | Laws, punishments |
| Goal | Harmony | State power |
| Method | Education | Coercion |

## Economic Thought

### Attitudes to Wealth

| View | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Not condemned | Wealth acceptable |
| Not primary | Virtue matters more |
| Proper means | Must be righteous |
| Distribution | Concern for inequality |

> "Wealth and rank attained through immoral means are to me as floating clouds."

### Agriculture vs. Commerce

| Sector | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Agriculture | Honorable, productive |
| Commerce | Suspect, unproductive |
| Officials | Above economic activity |
| Legacy | Merchant class low status |

### Work and Labor

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Manual labor | Not for gentlemen |
| Mental labor | Proper for junzi |
| Distinction | "Those who labor with minds govern; those who labor with strength are governed" |
| Effect | Scholar-official elite |
