# Fertile Crescent

The Fertile Crescent is the arc of relatively well-watered land stretching from the Persian Gulf through Mesopotamia, around the Syrian Desert, and down through the Levant to Egypt. Here, agriculture began, cities first arose, writing was invented, and the first empires formed. The region's geography—wild cereals, domesticable animals, and strategic position—gave it a head start that shaped world history.
