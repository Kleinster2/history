# Cyrus the Great

Cyrus II of Persia founded the largest empire the world had yet seenâ€”stretching from the Aegean to Central Asia. His conquests were remarkable not just for their scope but for their method: Cyrus practiced religious tolerance, respected local customs, and presented himself as a liberator rather than conqueror. The "Cyrus Cylinder" is sometimes called the first declaration of human rights.

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Method | Diverted Euphrates, entered under walls |
| Reception | Welcomed as liberator |

| Jews | Allowed return to Jerusalem |
| Propaganda | Presented as chosen by gods |

## The Cyrus Cylinder

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Object | Clay cylinder, Akkadian text |
| Content | Cyrus's justification for conquest |
| Claims | Restored peace, freed peoples |
| Modern interpretation | "First human rights charter" (exaggerated) |
| Reality | Royal propaganda, but unusual tolerance |
