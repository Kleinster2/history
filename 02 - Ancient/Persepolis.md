# Persepolis

Persepolis was the ceremonial capital of the Achaemenid Empire and the physical center of its tribute economy. Built to impress and to receive, this city existed primarily to stage the annual presentation of tribute from the empire's subject nationsâ€”a theatrical display of economic extraction carved in stone and enacted in ritual.
