# Akkadian Empire

The Akkadian Empire, founded by Sargon of Akkad around 2334 BCE, was the world's first empire—a multi-ethnic political entity ruling over diverse peoples and territories. For approximately 180 years, Akkadian kings dominated Mesopotamia and beyond, establishing patterns of imperial rule that influenced all subsequent Near Eastern empires.

## Empire Structure

### Territory

| Region | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Akkad (central) | Heartland |
| Sumer (south) | Conquered cities |
| Upper Mesopotamia | Directly controlled |
| Elam | Vassal/contested |
| Syria | Trade and influence |
| Anatolia | Trade connections |

### Administration

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| King | Divine or semi-divine ruler |
| Royal family | Governors of key cities |
| Ensi | City governors (often local) |
| Scribes | Akkadian language bureaucracy |
| Army | Permanent military force |

|--------|-------------|
