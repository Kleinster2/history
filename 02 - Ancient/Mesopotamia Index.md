# Mesopotamia

Mesopotamia—"the land between the rivers"—refers to the region between the Tigris and Euphrates rivers in modern-day Iraq and Syria. Often called the "cradle of civilization," it witnessed the emergence of the world's first cities, writing systems, law codes, and empires.

## Civilizations

### Sumer (c. 4500-2004 BCE)
- [[Sumer]] - The first civilization
- City-states: Ur, Uruk, Eridu, Lagash, Nippur
- Invented writing, wheel, plow
- Ziggurats and temple economies

### Akkadian Empire (2334-2154 BCE)
- [[Akkadian Empire]] - First world empire
- Founded by Sargon of Akkad
- Semitic rulers over Sumerian cities
- Model for later empires

### Babylon (1894-539 BCE)
- [[Babylon]] - Cultural and political center
- Old Babylonian: Hammurabi's laws
- Neo-Babylonian: Nebuchadnezzar II
- Hanging Gardens (legendary)

### Assyria (2500-609 BCE)
- [[Assyria]] - Military superpower
- Northern Mesopotamian kingdom
- Brutal but effective conquest
- Library of Ashurbanipal

## Themes

### Writing & Literature
- [[Cuneiform]] - Wedge-shaped writing
- [[Epic of Gilgamesh]] - First great literature
- Administrative records
- Scientific and medical texts

### Religion & Cosmology
- [[Mesopotamian Religion]] - Gods and temples
- Anu (sky), Enlil (wind), Enki (water)
- Inanna/Ishtar (love and war)
- Marduk (Babylon's patron)
- Afterlife as gloomy underworld

### Law & Society
- [[Code of Hammurabi]] - Famous law code
- [[Mesopotamian Society]] - Social hierarchy
- Temple and palace economies
- Slavery and debt bondage

### Technology
- [[Mesopotamian Technology]]
- Irrigation and agriculture
- Bronze metallurgy
- Wheel and chariot
- Mathematics (base-60)

## Economy

### Economic Systems

| System | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Temple economy | Redistribution through religious institutions |
| Palace economy | Royal control of resources |
| Private enterprise | Merchants, craftsmen, farmers |
| Mixed economy | All three coexisted |

### Key Economic Features

| Feature | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| Irrigation | Enabled surplus agriculture |
| Long-distance trade | Imported metals, stone, timber |
| Silver standard | Money of account |
| Credit and loans | Sophisticated banking |
| Written contracts | Legal commerce |
