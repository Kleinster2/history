# Greece Index

Ancient Greece gave the world its first systematic economic thoughtâ€”the very word "economy" (oikonomia) is Greek. From the Mycenaean palaces through the Classical city-states to the Hellenistic kingdoms, Greek economic systems evolved from palace redistribution to the most commercial economy of the ancient world.

## The Greek Economic Contribution

| Innovation | Significance |
|------------|--------------|
| Coinage spread | Standardized exchange medium |
| Agora markets | Public commercial space |
| Banking | Trapezitai, credit, lending |
| Economic theory | Xenophon, Aristotle analyzed economy |
| Commercial law | Maritime, contracts |
| The word "economy" | Oikonomia = household management |

## Major Poleis (City-States)

### Athens
- [[Athens]] - Commerce, silver, democracy
- Piraeus - The great port
- Silver mines (Laurion) - Financed navy, state

### Sparta
- [[Sparta]] - Militarized autarky
- Helot labor system
- Iron "money" (rejected commerce)

### Corinth
- Strategic isthmus location
- Pottery, shipbuilding
- Dual ports (east and west)

### Other Important Poleis
| Polis | Economic Character |
|-------|-------------------|
| Miletus | Ionian trade hub |
| Syracuse | Sicilian grain |
| Rhodes | Maritime commerce |
| Aegina | Early coinage |

## Key Themes

### Coinage
- [[Greek Coinage]] - Spread from Lydia
- Athenian "owls" - International currency
- Weight standards - Multiple systems

### Slavery
- [[Slavery Economics|Greek Slavery]] - Economic foundation
- Agricultural (helots)
- Household
- Mining (brutal conditions)
- Craftsmen

### Commerce
- Agora - Market center
- Emporion - Trading posts
- Trapeza - Banking tables

### Economic Thought
- [[Greek Economic Thought]] - First theorists
- Xenophon - *Oeconomicus*, *Ways and Means*
- Aristotle - Chrematistics, "natural" vs "unnatural"
- Plato - Ideal state economics
