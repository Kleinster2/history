# Persia Index

The Persian empires created the ancient world's most sophisticated systems of imperial administration and taxation before Rome. From the Achaemenid "King of Kings" to the Sasanid "King of Iran," Persian rulers governed vast territories through a combination of tribute extraction, road networks, standardized coinage, and local autonomy that allowed diverse economies to flourish under imperial oversight.

## The Persian Economic Achievement

| Feature | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| Royal Road | 2,700 km, communications infrastructure |
| Daric coinage | First large-scale gold coinage |
| Satrap system | Decentralized administration |
| Tribute system | Regularized extraction |
| Scale | First multi-continental empire |
| Duration | ~1,100 years of Persian empires |

## Major Empires

### Achaemenid Empire
- [[Achaemenid Empire]] - First Persian Empire
- Founded by Cyrus the Great
- Peak under Darius I
- Conquered by Alexander

### Parthian Empire
- [[Parthian Empire]] - Silk Road middlemen
- Arsacid dynasty
- Decentralized
- Rome's rival

### Sasanid Empire
- [[Sasanid Empire]] - Late antiquity power
- Zoroastrian state
- Centralized economy
- Fell to Arab conquest
