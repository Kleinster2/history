# Osiris

Osiris was ancient Egypt's most important godâ€”ruler of the underworld, judge of the dead, and symbol of resurrection. The Osiris myth shaped Egyptian beliefs about death and afterlife for three millennia. His cult center at [[Abydos]] attracted pilgrims, and every deceased Egyptian sought identification with Osiris. The god's widespread worship supported temple economies, festival industries, and the massive mortuary complex that characterized Egyptian society.
