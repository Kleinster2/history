# Alexandria

Alexandria was the greatest city of the Hellenistic worldâ€”a planned metropolis that became the Mediterranean's commercial and intellectual hub. Founded by Alexander, developed by the Ptolemies, and inherited by Rome, Alexandria dominated the grain trade, housed the legendary Library, and served as a crossroads of Greek, Egyptian, Jewish, and eventually Christian cultures.

## Overview

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Founded | 331 BCE by Alexander the Great |
| Location | Nile Delta, Mediterranean coast |
| Peak population | 300,000-500,000 |
| Status | Largest city in Mediterranean (for centuries) |
| End of ancient city | Arab conquest 641 CE |
