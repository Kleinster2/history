# Classical

The Classical period spans from the rise of the Greek city-states through the height of the Roman Empire—roughly 500 BCE to 300 CE. While [[Greece Index|Greece]] and [[Rome Index|Rome]] developed distinct political forms, they shared an economic foundation: coined money, slave labor, urban markets, and Mediterranean trade. The Classical world created the first large-scale integrated economy.

## Economic Limits

### Structural Constraints

| Constraint | Effect |
|------------|--------|
| Slavery | Reduced labor innovation |
| Elite disdain | Commerce low-status |
| Limited technology | No industrial breakthrough |
| Political instability | Periodic disruption |

### Why No Industrial Revolution?

| Factor | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Cheap slaves | No labor-saving incentive |
| Elite values | Land over commerce |
| State extraction | Surplus to military, monuments |
| Knowledge limits | Theory over application |
