# Venice

Venice was medieval Europe's most successful commercial city-state, dominating Mediterranean trade for centuries. Built on lagoon islands with no agricultural hinterland, Venice had to trade to surviveâ€”and became fabulously wealthy doing so. Its innovations in commerce, finance, and governance influenced the development of capitalism.

## Overview

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Founded | Traditionally 697 CE |
| Peak | 13th-15th centuries |
| Population | ~100,000+ at peak |
| Government | Republic (oligarchic) |
| Specialty | Eastern Mediterranean trade |
| Decline | After 1500 (Atlantic shift) |

|--------|-----------|
