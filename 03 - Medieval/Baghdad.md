# Baghdad

Baghdad was the largest and wealthiest city in the world during the Islamic Golden Age—a metropolis of perhaps one million people at its peak. Founded as the Abbasid capital in 762 CE, it became the center of a vast trade network connecting China to Spain, and a hub of learning, manufacturing, and finance that far surpassed contemporary Europe.
