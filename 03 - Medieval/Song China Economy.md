# Song China Economy

Song Dynasty China (960-1279) achieved the most advanced commercial economy of the medieval worldâ€”and arguably any pre-modern society. Paper money, proto-industrial production, massive urbanization, and sophisticated markets made Song China the economic wonder of its age. Its failure to industrialize despite these achievements is one of history's great puzzles.

## Paper Money

### The Innovation

First government-issued paper currency in history.

| Development | Date | Description |
|-------------|------|-------------|
| Private notes | 10th c. | Sichuan merchant notes |
| State takeover | 1024 | Government monopoly |
| National currency | 11th-12th c. | Widespread circulation |
| Overissue | 13th c. | Inflation problems |

### How It Worked

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Backing | Originally metal reserves |
| Issue | Government monopoly |
| Denominations | Various values |
| Acceptance | Required for taxes |
| Problem | Overissue temptation |

### Why Paper Money?

| Factor | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| Copper shortage | Not enough for coin demand |
| Trade volume | Too much to carry metal |
| Government capacity | State could issue credibly |
| Infrastructure | Printing technology ready |

### Comparison

| Song Paper Money | Modern Fiat Currency |
|------------------|---------------------|
| Government issued | Government issued |
| Initially backed | Initially backed (gold standard) |
| Became unbacked | Became unbacked |
| Inflation when overissued | Same risk |

## Why Not Industrial Revolution?

### The Puzzle

Song China had:
- High population
- Advanced technology
- Sophisticated markets
- Paper money
- Proto-industrial production
- Scientific knowledge

But did not industrialize. Why?

### Explanations

| Theory | Argument | Problem |
|--------|----------|---------|
| High-level equilibrium trap | Labor cheap, no labor-saving pressure | May explain |
| Resource availability | Less coal scarcity | Possibly |
| State intervention | Disrupted markets | Debatable |
| Mongol conquest | Interrupted development | External factor |
| Intellectual tradition | Less empirical | Contested |
| Geographic isolation | Less competition | Possibly relevant |

### The High-Level Equilibrium Trap

| European Path | Chinese Path |
|---------------|--------------|
| Labor scarce | Labor abundant |
| Wages high | Wages low |
| Labor-saving innovation | Labor-using solutions |
| Machines economical | Machines unnecessary |
| Industrial revolution | Continued intensive development |

### What Happened After

| Dynasty | Economic Direction |
|---------|-------------------|
| Yuan (Mongol) | Trade focus, disruption |
| Ming | Agricultural emphasis, withdrawal |
| Qing | Population growth, involution |
