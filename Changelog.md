- 2026-05-06: [[Soft-power inflections]] (`Themes/`) — new theme stub. Long-arc patterns of soft-power generation, drawdown, and transfer across cases: US Cold War apparatus (1945-65 generative phase, Marshall Plan / Hollywood / Fulbright / USIA), Soviet ideological projection (1945-89, institutional reach without generative popular-cultural surface), US Vietnam-era drawdown (1968-75, self-inflicted), unipolar peak (1989-2003), Iraq War / Abu Ghraib (2003-08), Trump I (2017-21), and the current 2025-26 Trump II + China algorithmic / open-source rise inflection. Three structural claims: soft power is path-dependent on hard-power phases (capital follows by 10-20 years); drawdowns are usually self-inflicted; replacements rarely look like predecessors (China's algorithmic / open-source vector ≠ Soviet state cultural diplomacy ≠ US Hollywood / consumer-export). Open question: durability of platform-algorithmic soft power vs entertainment-content soft power. - 2026-04-28: [[Tsingtao Brewery survival arc]] (`06 - Contemporary/`) — new topic note. The 90-year arc through which a German colonial brewery in Qingdao (founded August 15, 1903 as Germania-Brauerei Tsingtao Co., Ltd. by an Anglo-German Hong Kong-domiciled joint stock company) survived German colonial defeat (1916), Japanese occupation under Dai-Nippon Brewery (1916-1945), Nationalist receivership under the Tsui family (1945-1949), Communist nationalization without compensation (1949), three decades as state-owned enterprise through the Great Leap Forward and Cultural Revolution (1949-1972), 1972 introduction to US export market, and Deng-era reform-period privatization culminating in the June 1993 Hong Kong Stock Exchange listing — the first Chinese firm on HKEX. Identifies three patterns of economic persistence the arc illustrates: physical capital outlives sovereignty, tacit operational knowledge is the binding asset, foreign-currency-earning capacity is regime-protective.
