# Gilgamesh

Gilgamesh was a legendary king of Uruk who became the hero of the world's oldest known epic literature. Whether based on a historical figure or purely mythological, Gilgamesh's story—his quests, friendships, and confrontation with mortality—has resonated across millennia and continues to speak to fundamental human concerns.

## Historical Basis

### Evidence for Historical King

| Evidence | Details |
|----------|---------|
| Sumerian King List | Listed as 5th king of Uruk I dynasty |
| Reign | 126 years (legendary) |
| Other kings | Predecessors and successors are historical |
| Enmebaragesi | Contemporary king, historically attested |
| Building tradition | Credited with Uruk's walls |

### The Problem

| Issue | Discussion |
|-------|------------|
| No contemporary inscriptions | Unlike later kings |
| Legendary qualities | Divine parentage, superhuman feats |
| Reign length | Impossible 126 years |
| Conclusion | Possibly historical, heavily mythologized |

| Tablet | Content |
|--------|---------|
| I | Gilgamesh's tyranny, Enkidu's creation |

| VIII | Gilgamesh mourns |
| IX-X | Journey to Utnapishtim |
| XI | Flood story, plant of youth |
| XII | Appendix (Enkidu in underworld) |
