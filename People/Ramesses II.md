# Ramesses II (The Great)

Ramesses II was ancient Egypt's longest-reigning and most celebrated pharaoh, ruling for 66 years during the 19th Dynasty. His reign marked the pinnacle of New Kingdom power, characterized by massive construction projects that employed tens of thousands, military campaigns, international diplomacy, and unprecedented royal propaganda. His building program alone represents one of history's largest state-directed economic mobilizations.
