# Printing Revolution

Why did printing transform Europe but not China or Islam? Printing's revolutionary effect in Europe stemmed from institutional fragmentation—no authority could control the press, enabling intellectual competition. China invented movable type centuries earlier; the Islamic world rejected printing for 300 years. The same technology produced utterly different outcomes depending on institutional context.

| State | Could regulate production |

## Islamic Rejection

### The Delay

| Timeline | Status |
|----------|--------|
| 1485 | Ottoman sultan bans printing for Muslims |
| 1515 | Hebrew, Greek presses in Ottoman Empire |
| 1727 | First Muslim press, Istanbul |
| 1742 | Press closed |
| 19th century | Widespread adoption |

### Why Rejected?

| Factor | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| Ulema opposition | Threatened scribal class |
| Calligraphy | Religious, aesthetic value |
| Quran concerns | Must be handwritten? |
| Vested interests | Scribes, scholars |
| Centralized authority | Could enforce ban |

|--------|---------|

## The Deeper Point

Printing's revolutionary effect in Europe stemmed from [[Institutions and Progress|institutional fragmentation]]—no authority could control the press, enabling intellectual competition. China invented printing centuries earlier but couldn't exploit it—too many characters, state control, no competitive pressure. The [[Islamic World Technological Stagnation|Islamic world rejected]] printing for 300 years—ulema opposition, calligraphic tradition, centralized authority. Europe's fragmentation, paradoxically, enabled transformation: Protestant printers fled to tolerant cities, scientific journals crossed borders, no Index could suppress all ideas. The printing case reveals that [[Technology Diffusion Failures|technology alone doesn't transform]]—institutions determine whether technologies flourish or stagnate. The same invention (movable type) produced different outcomes based on character systems, political structures, religious contexts, and competitive dynamics. [[Innovation Economics|Innovation requires]] not just invention but institutional conditions that enable diffusion and application. Europe's chaos was its advantage; China's order was its constraint. [[Information Technology as Infrastructure|Information technology]] transforms only where institutions permit.
