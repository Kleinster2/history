# Gender & Economy

Gender—the socially constructed roles and expectations attached to sex—has profoundly shaped economic life throughout history. Women's labor, often unpaid or undervalued, has been essential to economies while gender norms have constrained women's economic participation. Understanding gender in economic history reveals how social structures shape production, distribution, and development.
