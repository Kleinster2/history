# Climate & History

Climate has shaped human history more profoundly than most narratives acknowledge. Ice ages determined human migration, rainfall patterns enabled or destroyed civilizations, and climate shifts contributed to the fall of empires. From the Medieval Warm Period's agricultural expansion to the Little Ice Age's famines, temperature and precipitation drove economic outcomes. Climate is not destiny, but it sets the constraints within which societies operate.
