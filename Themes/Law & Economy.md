# Law & Economy

Law and economy have been intertwined throughout history: legal systems define property, enforce contracts, regulate commerce, and shape economic behavior. From ancient law codes like [[Hammurabi]]'s to modern commercial law, legal frameworks enable and constrain economic activity. Understanding law's economic role illuminates how institutions shape development.
